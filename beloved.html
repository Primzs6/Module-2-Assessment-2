Cry, Beloved Country
Set in South Africa in October 1946, before the implementation of apartheid, this is the story of church minister Stephen Kumalo (James Earl Jones) who is requested from his village to Johannesburg.[1] There he discovers that his son Absalom has been arrested for the murder of a white man. The white man's father, James Jarvis (Richard Harris), supports apartheid. When the two meet, they come to unexpected understandings about their sons and their own humanity.
